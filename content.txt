Bet Tracker (Corporate / The "Market First")

Section 1 — TL;DR
Problem: 69% of users were switching between Sportsbet and AFL app to track the game outcome.
Action: Designed a first-to-market bet tracker that surfaces core stats directly within the "My Bets" view.

Outcome: 90% engagement rate for AFL punters and 70% of users now rate tracking as "important" or "very important".

Section 2 — Context
Team: Sports Betting Product.
Role: Sole Designer owning the project from exploration to production.
Constraints: High pressure to be first to market; limited by initial data provider templates.

Section 3 — The Messy Progression
The "V1" Intuition: We launched with two basic templates. It worked, but post-launch research was a wake-up call.
The Friction: Users loved the idea but didn't trust the data. They were confused about when it was actually "live" and were frustrated when a player got injured without the app reflecting it.
The Improved: Phase 2 wasn't about "new features"; it was about building trust. We added jersey numbers for recognition and "bench/injury" status so users could make informed cash-out decisions.

Section 4 — Outcome
90% engagement among AFL punters.Evolved from a "nice-to-have" feature to a core reason for app retention.

Wayfinding in Landscape (Start-up / The "Intuition" Phase)

Section 1 — TL;DR

Problem: 90% of Newtracs users were using the app in landscape while driving, but the UI was strictly portrait-optimized.

Action: Rapid audit and redesign of the landscape experience, prioritizing "glanceable" info and reachability.

Outcome: Created a comprehensive "in-car" experience without the luxury of a long research phase.

Section 2 — Context

Environment: Fast-paced start-up (Newtracs).
Timeline: 4 weeks.
Process: Zero external research. Straight up driven by internal team audit, intuition, and tight dev-loop testing.

Section 3 — Design Decisions

Spatial Memory: Anchored real-time info (speed, location) in the bottom left to match portrait habits.

Reachability: Grouped primary actions (Search, Route Builder) on the right edge for easy thumb-reach while the phone is docked.

IA Pivot: Replaced the bottom nav with a right-side drawer to save horizontal screen real estate.

Sports Homepage Experience

Section 1 — TL;DR

Problem: Current global IA nav introduces confusion and repetition across the app.

Action: Reduce options on global IA nav and surface personalisation content on homepage with filtering options

Outcome: Introduce new way of navigating and building bet across the app.

Section 2 — Context

Project type: Exploratory design.
Timeline: 2 days.
Process: Quick team ideation that brings to life in Figma and using Cursor AI to produce a full working prototype.

Section 3 — Design Decisions

Homepage to be Your Home - No more generic content, surface relevant big event and content based on your history.

Crown the search - Tap into users with "I know what I'm looking for mindset".

Contextual Betslip - Allow bet building anywhere on the platform, not just within "Bet Slip" space.

Feed Profile Creation

Section 1 — TL;DR

Problem: 3/10 users landed on Feed creating a profile

Action: Proposed an auto-profile creation (Reddit-style), which was rejected; pivoted to a fast profile creation flow with suggested username and avatar.

Outcome: 100% usability test completion, though the project lacked initial user validation.

Section 2 — Context
Team: Customer to Customer team.
Role: Exploratory designer.
Constraints: Limited tech feasibility in the space and lack of initial user research of the product.

Section 3 — The Messy Reality

Stakeholder Friction: This project is a lesson in "disagree and commit." My initial recommendation for full automation was blocked by the product team.


The Workaround: I had to find a middle ground—uplifting the current UI with pre-selected avatars to reduce friction while keeping stakeholders happy.


- This was done without initial user research—strictly moving fast to hit OKRs.
